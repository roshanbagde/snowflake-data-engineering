# 4. Positions and Trading

What it means to "have a position," and the words around buying and selling.

---

### Trade
A single act of buying or selling.
📌 *Example:* "I made a trade — bought 10 Apple shares."

### Order
An instruction you give your broker to buy or sell.
- **Market order:** "Buy now at whatever the current price is." (Fast, price not guaranteed.)
- **Limit order:** "Buy only at $189 or lower." (Price guaranteed, but might not execute.)
🧠 *Analogy:* Market order = "grab it now." Limit order = "only if it's on sale at my price."

### Buy / Sell
- **Buy:** you pay money to own the instrument.
- **Sell:** you give up the instrument in exchange for money.

### Position
What you currently **own (or owe)** in a particular instrument. Having a position = being "in the
game" on that instrument.
📌 *Example:* "I have a position of 100 Apple shares." If you sell all of them, you have "no position"
(you're **flat**).
🧠 *Analogy:* Your current holdings in a board game — the pieces you have on the board right now.

### Long vs Short (very important pair)
- **Long:** you **own** it and profit if the price **goes up**. (The normal way.)
  📌 "I'm long 100 Apple" = I own 100 shares and want the price to rise.
- **Short:** you **borrow and sell** something you don't own, hoping to buy it back **cheaper** later.
  You profit if the price **goes down**.
  🧠 *Analogy:* You borrow your friend's concert ticket, sell it for $100 today, and plan to buy a
  ticket back for $70 next week to return to them — pocketing $30. You profit if ticket prices fall.
  ❓ *Risk:* If the price *rises* instead, shorting can lose a lot — there's no limit to how high a
  price can go.

### Flat
Having no position — you've bought and sold equal amounts, so you own nothing in that instrument.

### Quantity (Qty) / Size
How many units are in the trade or position.
📌 *Example:* Quantity = 100 shares.

### Notional / Notional Value
The total cash value of a position = price × quantity.
📌 *Example:* 100 shares × $190 = **$19,000 notional**.
🧠 *Analogy:* Not "how many apples," but "the total dollar size of your apple pile."
❓ *Why it matters:* It's the headline size of your exposure — how much money is really riding on it.

### Exposure
How much you stand to gain or lose from price moves in something.
📌 *Example:* If you're long $19,000 of Apple, you have $19,000 of exposure to Apple's price.

### Holding
An instrument you currently own. Your **holdings** = everything in your portfolio.

### Cost Basis / Average Price
The average price you paid for a position. Used to figure out profit later.
📌 *Example:* Bought 50 shares at $180 and 50 at $200 → cost basis = $190 average.

### Execution / Fill
When your order actually gets matched with a counterparty and the trade happens.
📌 *Example:* "My order to buy at $189 got **filled**." A partial fill = only some of it traded.

### Counterparty
The other side of your trade — whoever sold to you (or bought from you).

### Settlement (intro)
The behind-the-scenes process where the shares and the cash actually change hands a day or two after
the trade. (Full detail in file 09 — "T+1/T+2".)
