# 1. Market Basics

The foundation. What is actually being bought and sold, and where.

---

### Company / Issuer
A business (like Apple or a local bank). To grow, it often needs money. One way to get it is to
sell small ownership pieces of itself to the public.
🧠 *Analogy:* A pizza shop owner who needs cash cuts the business into 100 slices and sells some.

### Share / Stock / Equity
A **share** is one small piece of ownership in a company. **Stock** and **equity** are just other
words for the same thing (the words are used interchangeably).
🧠 *Analogy:* One slice of that pizza-shop ownership.
📌 *Example:* If a company has 1,000 shares and you own 10, you own 1% of the company.
❓ *Why it matters:* If the company does well, your share becomes more valuable, and you can sell it
for more than you paid.

> **Stock vs Share — tiny difference:** "Stock" is the general concept ("I own Apple stock").
> "Share" is the countable unit ("I own 10 shares"). Day to day, people mix them freely.

### Shareholder / Investor
A person or organization that owns shares. As a part-owner, you may get a slice of profits
(see *dividend*) and a vote on big company decisions.

### Stock Market
The whole system where shares are bought and sold.
🧠 *Analogy:* "The housing market" is all home buying/selling everywhere. The stock market is the
same idea, but for shares.

### Exchange
A specific, organized marketplace where trades actually happen — e.g. **NYSE** and **NASDAQ** (USA),
**NSE/BSE** (India), **LSE** (London).
🧠 *Analogy:* A farmers' market building where buyers and sellers gather. The stock market is the
concept; an exchange is an actual venue.

### Ticker / Symbol
A short code that identifies a company's stock on an exchange.
📌 *Example:* Apple = **AAPL**, Microsoft = **MSFT**, Tesla = **TSLA**.
🧠 *Analogy:* A nickname so people don't have to say the full company name every time.

### Index (plural: indices)
A single number that tracks how a *group* of stocks is doing overall.
📌 *Example:* The **S&P 500** tracks 500 big US companies. The **Nasdaq-100**, the **Dow**, the
**Nifty 50** are other indices.
🧠 *Analogy:* A class's average test score — one number that summarizes how the whole group did.
❓ *Why it matters:* When the news says "the market went up 1% today," they usually mean an index.

### Bull Market vs Bear Market
- **Bull market:** prices generally going **up**, optimism. 🐂 (horns point up)
- **Bear market:** prices generally going **down**, pessimism. 🐻 (claws swipe down)

### IPO (Initial Public Offering)
The first time a company sells its shares to the public on an exchange.
🧠 *Analogy:* The pizza shop's "grand opening" of selling ownership slices to anyone.

### Portfolio
The full collection of investments a person or fund owns.
🧠 *Analogy:* Your whole basket of groceries, not just one item.
📌 *Example:* A portfolio might hold 10 Apple shares, 5 Tesla shares, and some bonds.
