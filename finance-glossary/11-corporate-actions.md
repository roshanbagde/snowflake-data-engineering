# 11. Corporate Actions

A **corporate action** is any event where a company does something that affects its shares or
shareholders. The back office has to process these carefully so records and P&L stay correct.

🧠 *Big analogy:* Things the "pizza shop" does that change your slices — paying you a bonus, cutting
each slice in half, or buying some slices back.

---

### Dividend
A share of the company's profit paid to shareholders, usually as cash.
📌 *Example:* A company pays $1 per share. Own 100 shares → receive $100.
🧠 *Analogy:* The shop owner sharing this month's profit with all the part-owners.
❓ *Why it matters:* It's income you earn just for holding the stock, separate from price gains.

### Dividend Yield
The dividend as a percentage of the share price.
📌 *Example:* $2 annual dividend on a $50 stock = 4% yield.

### Ex-Dividend Date
The cutoff date: you must own the stock **before** this date to receive the upcoming dividend.

### Stock Split
The company divides each share into more shares, lowering the price per share — but your total value
stays the same.
📌 *Example:* A 2-for-1 split: 10 shares at $200 become 20 shares at $100. Still $2,000.
🧠 *Analogy:* Cutting each pizza slice in two — more slices, same amount of pizza.
❓ *Why it matters:* Makes pricey shares look more affordable; doesn't make you richer by itself.

### Reverse Split
The opposite — combining shares into fewer, higher-priced ones.
📌 *Example:* 1-for-10: 100 shares at $1 become 10 shares at $10.

### Share Buyback / Repurchase
The company buys its own shares back from the market, reducing the number out there.
🧠 *Analogy:* The shop owner buying back ownership slices — each remaining slice now represents a
bigger piece of the company.
❓ *Why it matters:* Fewer shares can make each remaining share more valuable.

### Rights Issue
Existing shareholders get the right to buy new shares, often at a discount, before others.
🧠 *Analogy:* Loyalty members get first dibs on a sale.

### Merger & Acquisition (M&A)
- **Merger:** two companies combine into one.
- **Acquisition:** one company buys another.
🧠 *Analogy:* Two shops joining into one, or a big chain buying a small shop.
❓ *Why it matters:* Your shares may convert into the new/acquiring company's shares or cash.

### Spin-off
A company splits off part of itself into a brand-new separate company, and you get shares in it.
🧠 *Analogy:* The bakery section of a supermarket becomes its own standalone bakery business, and
existing owners get a stake in it.

### Coupon (for bonds)
The regular interest payment a bond pays its holder. (The bond world's version of a dividend.)
📌 *Example:* A bond with a 5% coupon on $1,000 pays $50 per year.
